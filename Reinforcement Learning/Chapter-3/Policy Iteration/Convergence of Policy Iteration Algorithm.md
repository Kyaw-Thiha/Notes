# Convergence of Policy Iteration Algorithm
#rl/planning/policy-iteration

> Let $(\pi_{k})_{k \geq 0}$ be the sequence generated by the [[Policy Iteration|PI algorithm]].
> - For all $k$, we have that $V^{\pi_{k+1}} \geq V^{\pi_{k}}$ with equality if and only if $V^{\pi_{k}} = V^{*}$.
> - $\lim_{ k \to \infty }||V^{\pi_{k}} - V^{*}||_{\infty} = 0$
> - If the set of policies is finite, the [[Policy Iteration|PI algorithm]] converges in a finite number of iterations.

---
## Proof
> The basic idea behind the proof is that 
> - either we can strictly improve the policy, 
> - or if we cannot, we are already at the optimal policy.

We shall prove each of the claims consecutively.

**Claim-1**: $V^{\pi_{k}} \geq V^{\pi_{k}} \iff V^{\pi_{k}} = V^{*}$
By [[Policy Improvement Theorem]], we already know that $V^{\pi_{k+1}} \geq V^{\pi_{k}}$.
So we need to prove $V^{\pi_{k+1}} = V^{\pi_{k}} \iff V^{\pi_{k}} = V^{*}$.

**Proof of** $V^{\pi_{k+1}} = V^{\pi_{k}} \implies V^{\pi_{k}} = V^{*}$
Suppose that instead of strict equality, we have an equality of
$$
V^{\pi_{k+1}} = V^{\pi_{k}}
$$
Applying $T^{\pi_{k+1}}$ to both sides, 
$$
T^{\pi_{k+1}} \ V^{\pi_{k+1}}
= T^{\pi_{k+1}} V^{\pi_{k}}
$$
As $T^{\pi_{k+1}} V^{\pi_{k}} = T^{*}V^{\pi_{k}}$ by the definition of the [[Policy Iteration|PI algorithm]],
$$
\begin{align}
T^{\pi_{k+1}} \ V^{\pi_{k+1}} 
= T^{\pi_{k+1}} V^{\pi_{k}} 
= T^{*} V^{\pi_{k}} 
= T^{*} V^{\pi_{k+1}}
\end{align}
$$
where in the last step, we used $V^{\pi_{k+1}} = V^{\pi_{k}}$ again.

By these inequalities, we have
$$
T^{\pi_{k+1}} V^{\pi_{k+1}}
= T^{*} V^{\pi_{k+1}}
$$
As $V^{\pi_{k+1}}$ is the [[Value Function|value function]] of $\pi_{k+1}$, it satisfies the [[Bellman Equation for Value Function|Bellman Equation]] $T^{\pi_{k+1}} V^{\pi_{k+1}} = V^{\pi_{k+1}}$.
Therefore, we also have
$$
V^{\pi_{k+1}} = T^{*}V^{\pi_{k+1}}
$$
This means that $V^{\pi_{k+1}}$ is a [[Fixed Point]] of $T^{*}$.
But recall that by [[Uniqueness of Fixed Point]] theorem, the fixed point of $T^{*}$ is unique and is equal to $V^{*}$.

So, we must have that
$$
V^{\pi_{k+1}} = V^{*}
$$
