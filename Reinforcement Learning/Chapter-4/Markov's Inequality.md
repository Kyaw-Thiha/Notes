# Markov's Inequality
#stats
For a non-negative random variable $Z$, for any $\epsilon>0$ we have
$$
\boxed{ \ 
\mathbb{P}\{ Z > \epsilon \}
\leq \frac{\mathbb{E}[Z]}{\epsilon}
\ }
$$

> The probability that a non-negative $r.v.$ $Z$ is much larger than its expectation is decreasing.

For instance,
$$
\mathbb{P}\{ Z > k \ \mathbb{E}[Z] \} \leq 
\frac{1}{k}
$$
