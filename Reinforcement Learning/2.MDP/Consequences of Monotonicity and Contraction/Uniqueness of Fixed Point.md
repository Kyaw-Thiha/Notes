# Uniqueness of Fixed Point
#rl/bellman-equation/operator 
**Theorem**: The operators $T^{\pi}$ and $T^{*}$ have a unique [[Fixed Point|fixed points]], denoted by $V^{\pi}$(or $Q^{\pi}$) and $V^{*}$(or $Q^{*}$)
$$
\begin{align}
&V^{\pi} = T^{\pi} V^{\pi}
\ ; \quad Q^{\pi} = T^{\pi}Q^{\pi} \\[6pt]

&V^{*} = T^{*} V^{*}
\ ; \quad Q^{*} = T^{*}Q^{*}
\end{align}
$$

Moreover they can be computed from any $V_{0} \in \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{S})$ or $Q_{0} \in \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{S} \times \mathcal{A})$ by iteratively computing
$$
\begin{align}
&V_{k+1} \leftarrow T^{\pi}V_{k}
\ ; \quad Q_{k+1} \leftarrow T^{\pi}Q_{k} \\[6pt]
&V_{k+1} \leftarrow T^{*}V_{k}
\ ; \quad Q_{k+1} \leftarrow T^{*}Q_{k}
\end{align}
$$
for $k=0,1, \dots$, we have that 
- $V_{k} \to V^{\pi}$ and $Q_{k} \to Q^{\pi}$ for $T^{\pi}$
- $V_{k} \to V^{*}$ and $Q_{k} \to Q^{*}$ for $T^{*}$

---
**Proof**
Consider the space of bounded functions $\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{S})$ with the [[Metric|metric]] $d$ based on the [[Infinity Norm|supremum norm]],
$$
d_{\infty}(V_{1}, V_{2})
= ||V_{1} - V_{2}||_{\infty}
= \sup_{s \in \mathcal{S}} |V_{1}(s) - V_{2}(s)|
$$
The space $(\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{S}), \ d_{\infty})$ is a complete metric space.

It is proven [[Contraction of Bellman Operator|here]] that for any $\pi$, operator $T^{\pi}$ is a $\gamma$-[[Contraction Mapping|contraction]]. 
It is also proven that $T^{*}$ has the same property too.

By the [[Banach Fixed Point Theorem]], 
- each of these operators have a unique fixed point.
- any sequence $(V_{k})$ with $V_{0} \in \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{S})$ and $V_{k+1} \leftarrow T^{\pi} V_{k}$ $(k=0,1,\dots)$ is convergent, meaning $\lim_{ k \to \infty } ||V_{k} - V^{\pi}|| = 0$.
- the same is true for sequence generated by repeatedly applying $T^{*}$.
