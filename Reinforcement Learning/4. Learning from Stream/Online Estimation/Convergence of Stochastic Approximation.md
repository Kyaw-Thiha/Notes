# Convergence of Stochastic Approximation
#rl/stochastic-approximation

> Let $(\theta_{t})$ be the sequence generated by this [[#Generalization|SA procedure]].
> Assume that 
> 1. $(Step \ size)$ The step sizes $\alpha_{t}(i)$ for $i=1, \dots, d$ are non-negative and satisfy

$$
\sum ^{\infty}_{t=0} \alpha_{t}(i) = \infty
\ \quad \ 
\sum ^{\infty}_{t=0} \alpha^{2}_{t}(i) < \infty
$$
> 2. $(Noise)$ The noise $\eta_{t}(i)$ satisfies the [[#Noise Assumptions|noise assumptions]].
> 3. The mapping $L$ is a [[Contraction Mapping|contraction]] $w.r.t$ $||\cdot||_{\infty}$ with the [[Fixed Point|fixed point]] of $\theta^{*}$.
> 
> Then, $\theta_{t}$ converges to $\theta^{*}$ almost surely.
## Convergence 
Let $\theta \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$ and $L: \mathbb{R}^{d} \to \mathbb{R}^{d}$.
Suppose we want to find [[Fixed Point|fixed-point]] of an [[Bellman Operator|operator]] $L$:
$$
L\theta = \theta
$$

Consider the following iterative update:
$$
\theta_{t+1} \leftarrow (1-\alpha) \ \theta_{t}
+ \alpha \ L\theta_{t}
$$
If $L$ is [[Lipschitzness|c-Lipschitz]] with $c<1$ and $\alpha$ is small enough $\left( 0 < \alpha < \frac{2}{1 - c} \right)$, 
the update will converge.

---
### Showing the Convergence
Note that the above iteration is equivalent to having
$$
\theta_{t+1} \leftarrow L'\theta_{t}
$$
where the new operator is
$$
L': \theta \mapsto [ \ (1-\alpha)\mathbf{I} 
+ \alpha L \ ] \ \theta
$$
for any $\theta_{1}, \theta_{2} \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$, this new operator satisfies
$$
\begin{align}
|| L'\theta_{1} - L'\theta_{2} ||
&\leq (1-\alpha) || \theta_{1} - \theta_{2} ||
+ \alpha ||L\theta_{1} - L\theta_{2}|| \\[6pt]
&\leq [ \ (1 - \alpha) + \alpha c \ ]
\ ||\theta_{1} - \theta_{2}||
\end{align}
$$

So if $|(1 - \alpha) + \alpha c| < 1$, then it satisfies $0 < \alpha < \frac{2}{1-c}$.
Hence, $L'$ is a [[Contraction Mapping|contraction mapping]].
By the [[Banach Fixed Point Theorem]], the iteration converges.

---
### Adding in Noise
Suppose we have $L\theta_{t} + \eta_{t}$ with $\eta_{t} \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$ being a zero-mean noise.
We then perform 
$$
\theta_{t+1} \leftarrow (1 - \alpha_{t}) \theta_{t}
+ \alpha_{t} (L\theta_{t} + \eta_{t})
$$
Note that this iteration process is similar to [[Online Estimator of Mean of Random Variable]]. 

As discussed [[Stochastic Approximation (SA) Analysis|here]], choosing a constant [[Learning Rate|learning rate]] $\alpha$ would leads to the variance of the estimate $\alpha_{t}$ not converging to $0$.
This is why we use an iteration-dependant $\alpha_{t}$.

---
### Generalization
Let's allow some dimensions of $\theta$ to be updated while others remain the same, as is the case with asynchronous update in [[Q-Learning]] and [[Temporal Difference Learning for Policy Evaluation(TD)|TD Learning]].

At time $t$, the $i^{th}$ component of $\theta_{t}$ is updated as
$$
\theta_{t+1}(i) \leftarrow (1 - \alpha_{t}(i))
\ \theta_{t}(i) + \alpha_{t}(i)
\ [ \ (L\theta_{t})(i) + \eta_{t}(i) \ ]
$$
with $\alpha_{t}(j) = 0$ for $j\neq i$.

Let $F_{t}$ be the history of the algorithm up to time $t$:
$$
F_{t} 
= \{ \theta_{0}, \ \theta_{1}, \ \dots, \ \theta_{t} \}
\ \cup \ 
\{ \eta_{0}, \ \eta_{1}, \ \dots, \ \eta_{t-1} \}
\ \cup \ 
\{ \alpha_{0}, \ \alpha_{1}, \ \dots, \ \alpha_{t} \}
$$
Note that $\eta_{t}$ is not included because it has not happened yet.

---
### Noise Assumptions
1. For every $i$ and $t$, we have $\mathbb{E}[\eta_{t}(i) \mid F_{t}] = 0$.
2. Given any norm $||\cdot||$ on $\mathbb{R}^{d}$, there exists constants $c_{1}, c_{2}$ such that for all $i$ and $t$, we have
$$
\mathbb{E}[ \ | \eta_{t}(i) |^{2} \mid F_{t} \ ]
\leq c_{1} + c_{2} \ ||\theta_{t}||^{2}
$$
- $1^{st}$ assumption indicates that noise $\eta_{t}$ should be zero-mean.
- $2^{nd}$ assumption asserts that the variance of the noise is not too much.
